Gajar Halwa

Gajar Halwa is a traditional Indian dessert made by slow-cooking grated carrots with milk, sugar, and ghee. Enhanced with cardamom and garnished with nuts and raisins, it is a rich and flavorful sweet treat often enjoyed during festivals and special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg carrots, grated
  • 4 cups full-fat milk
  • 1 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 4 tablespoons ghee
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1/4 cup chopped nuts (almonds, cashews, pistachios)
  • 2 tablespoons raisins

Steps

  1. Heat ghee in a large pan or kadhai over medium heat. Add the grated carrots and saut√© for 8-10 minutes until softened.
  2. Pour in the milk and simmer on low heat, stirring occasionally, until the carrots absorb the milk and the mixture thickens.
  3. Stir in the sugar and continue to cook, allowing the sugar to dissolve and caramelize slightly.
  4. Mix in the cardamom powder, chopped nuts, and raisins. Cook for another 5-7 minutes, stirring frequently.
  5. Serve warm or chilled, garnished with additional nuts if desired.

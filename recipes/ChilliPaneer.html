Chilli Paneer

Chilli Paneer is a popular Indo-Chinese dish made with crispy fried paneer cubes tossed in a spicy, tangy, and flavorful sauce. Combining the crunch of stir-fried vegetables and the richness of paneer, it’s a perfect appetizer or side dish. Often served with fried rice or noodles, it’s a crowd favorite for spice lovers.

Ingredients

  • 200 grams paneer, cubed
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chili sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tomato ketchup
  • 1 teaspoon vinegar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch (dissolved in water for slurry)
  • Spring onions for garnish

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix cornstarch, all-purpose flour, black pepper powder, and a pinch of salt. Add water to make a smooth batter.
  2. Dip the paneer cubes in the batter and deep-fry them until golden and crisp. Remove and set aside.
  3. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan or wok over medium heat. Add minced garlic, ginger, and green chilies, and sauté until fragrant.
  4. Add diced onions and bell peppers. Stir-fry on high heat for 2-3 minutes until slightly softened but still crunchy.
  5. Mix in soy sauce, chili sauce, tomato ketchup, and vinegar. Stir well to coat the vegetables in the sauce.
  6. Add the cornstarch slurry to the pan and stir continuously to thicken the sauce.
  7. Add the fried paneer cubes and toss gently to coat them in the sauce evenly.
  8. Garnish with chopped spring onions and serve hot with fried rice or noodles.
